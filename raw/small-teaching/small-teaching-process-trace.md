## Running notes
- Three-part structure: Part I Knowledge (Predicting, Retrieving, Interleaving), Part II Understanding (Connecting, Practicing, Explaining), Part III Inspiration (Belonging, Motivating, Learning).
- Second edition (2021) substantially revised two chapters: "Self-Explaining" → "Explaining" (broadened scope) and "Growing" → "Belonging" (shifted from growth mindset focus to belonging/asset-based teaching).
- Each chapter follows a consistent format: Introduction → In Theory → Models → Principles → Quick Tips → Conclusion.
- Three forms of small teaching: brief 5-10 min classroom activities, one-time course interventions, small modifications to course design/communication.
- Selection criteria for principles: (1) foundation in learning sciences research, (2) demonstrated positive impact in real educational settings, (3) personally observed by the author.
- Ch. 1 core argument: prediction before instruction enhances learning even when wrong, through prior knowledge activation, generation effect, and emotional investment (surprise). Four models: activating prior knowledge, polling predictions, prediction-exposure-feedback, closing predictions.
- Part I intro: Bloom's taxonomy misread as hierarchy to skip past; Willingham argues factual knowledge is prerequisite for higher-order thinking. Strong convergence with existing wiki threads on LTM and knowledge foundations.
- Ch. 2 core argument: the testing/retrieval effect — active recall beats restudy. Four models: opening questions, closing questions (Faculty Feedback Form), online retrieval via conditional release + large banks + spaced due dates, and the "living syllabus" as retrieval cue. Three principles: frequency matters, align practice with assessment, require thinking.
- Ch. 2 minor factual slip: Small Teaching p. 44 locates the Agarwal/Bain school in Columbia, Missouri; Make It Stick (same study) says Columbia, Illinois. Noted in columbia-il-science-quizzing.md.
- Cross-chapter: "memory is the residue of thought" (Willingham) surfaced as a general insight connecting Ch. 1 (prediction works because it forces thought) and Ch. 2 (retrieval works for the same reason). Promoted to its own entry and will likely recur in later chapters.
