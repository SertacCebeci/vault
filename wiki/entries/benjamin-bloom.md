# Benjamin Bloom

Psychologist who authored *Taxonomy of Educational Objectives* (1956), which became a foundational framework for educators worldwide. The taxonomy categorizes cognitive learning into six levels — Knowledge, Comprehension, Application, Analysis, Synthesis, and Evaluation — and is often depicted as a pyramid. While immensely influential, the pyramid representation has been widely misread as implying knowledge is a low-value rung to be skipped past, when in fact the taxonomy shows that [[knowledge-as-foundation]] is prerequisite for all higher-order thinking.

## Key contributions
- Bloom's Taxonomy of Educational Objectives — the six-level cognitive framework that shaped curriculum design and assessment globally
