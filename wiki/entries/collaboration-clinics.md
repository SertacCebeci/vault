# Collaboration Clinics

Periodic ten-minute sessions where students reflect on and troubleshoot their group dynamics. Clinics teach students to handle uncomfortable social situations themselves rather than ignoring problems or relying on the teacher to solve them.

## How it works

As weeks pass and group problems brew, the teacher conducts a collaboration clinic. Groups may be mixed up beforehand so students can speak more candidly. The key rule: focus on dysfunctional behaviors, not names. Each student submits a written, process-focused reflection on how their group is functioning — written responses tend to produce greater honesty, especially with an assurance of confidentiality. Simple prompts work: "What worked?" "What didn't?" "What improvements can be made?"

The teacher uses these reflections to identify common themes and develop mini-lessons targeting specific issues (e.g., free-riding, domineering members). Groups then brainstorm strategies to improve the situation, and humorous solutions are welcomed. The teacher collects strategies on the board and may add suggestions.

The emotional processing that emerges — students articulating unfair treatment, confronting consequences of leaving work to others or dominating a group — often teaches more about accountability and boundaries than any direct lecture could.
