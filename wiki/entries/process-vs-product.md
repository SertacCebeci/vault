# Process vs Product

**Process** means the flow of time and the habits and actions associated with that flow — "I'm going to spend 20 minutes working." **Product** is an outcome — "I need to finish this homework assignment." The key anti-[[procrastination]] insight: it is the *product* that triggers the pain that causes procrastination, because thinking about finishing a task can feel overwhelming. Focusing on process removes that trigger.

## Elaboration

When you think about a product ("I have to answer these five questions"), you unconsciously evaluate the effort involved, which activates pain centers and triggers avoidance. But committing to a process ("I will work for one [[pomodoro-technique|Pomodoro]]") is painless — who cares whether you finish? The zombie habitual part of the brain actually *likes* processes because it can march mindlessly along without the anxiety of "am I getting closer to finishing?"

The surfer analogy captures this: a surfer in the moment is focused on the process of riding the wave, not on the accomplishment of having surfed it. This allows complete engagement with the task. The practical strategy is to avoid focusing on product entirely — set up a process-oriented environment (timer, quiet space, phone away), focus on each small chunk of time, and let completion take care of itself.

This also connects to the planner journal approach: writing down process-oriented tasks ("study Chapter 3 for two Pomodoros") rather than product-oriented tasks ("finish Chapter 3") fundamentally changes your relationship with the work.
