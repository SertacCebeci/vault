# Terrence Sejnowski

Computational neuroscientist and co-director of a science and learning center sponsored by the National Science Foundation, based at the Salk Institute in La Jolla. Co-instructor of the "Learning How to Learn" MOOC, providing the neuroscience foundation for the course's practical advice. His lectures cover the biological basis of learning: synaptic formation, brain imaging, the role of the hippocampus in [[long-term-memory]], the [[neuromodulators]] (acetylcholine, dopamine, serotonin), astrocytes, and new imaging techniques showing synaptic turnover.

## Key contributions

- Provided neuroscience grounding for practical learning strategies → [[long-term-memory]], [[neuromodulators]], [[sleep-and-learning]]
- Presented research on synaptic plasticity and brain imaging → new techniques showing synapses less than a micron in diameter
- Highlighted Einstein's brain research: more astrocytes, not more neurons
- Emphasized that formal education focuses on the product of learning, not the process — the course aims to correct that imbalance
