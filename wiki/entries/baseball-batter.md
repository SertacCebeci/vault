# The Baseball Batter

**Concepts illustrated:** [[mental-models]], [[chunking]]

A baseball batter has less than an instant to identify whether an incoming pitch is a curveball, changeup, or fastball. He does this by building distinct mental models — learned from subtle cues like the pitcher's wind-up, the ball's spin, its seam rotation. These models connect to knowledge about batting stance, strike zone, and game situation (runners on base, outs). In a great at-bat, all these interconnected models fire seamlessly and the batter drives it through a hole. This is expertise: not one big skill, but an interconnected web of mental models.

## Why it matters

Illustrates how [[mental-models]] are built from [[chunking]] — many small, practiced knowledge units wired together into fluid performance.
