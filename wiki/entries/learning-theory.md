# Learning Theory

## Lead

The cognitive science of how humans acquire durable, transferable knowledge and skill — covering encoding, retrieval, consolidation, generalization, expertise, and the regularities that distinguish strategies that feel productive from strategies that actually work.

## Scope

This domain covers the cognitive-mechanism level of learning. It is concerned with empirical regularities about how the mind takes in information, stabilizes it, retrieves it, and transfers it across contexts. Core topics include:

- Encoding, retrieval, and consolidation as memory operations.
- Spacing, interleaving, retrieval practice, and the mechanism by which "desirable difficulties" produce stronger learning than fluency-feeling alternatives.
- Transfer — near, far, surface vs. deep features.
- The distinction between biologically primary and secondary knowledge (Geary).
- Working memory, chunking, schema acquisition.
- Expertise development and deliberate practice.
- The metacognitive failures that mislead learners (illusions of competence, fluency illusion).

## Out of scope

- The brain-level implementation of these mechanisms — neurons, regions, neuromodulators, plasticity. → [[neuroscience]].
- The teacher-side practice of orchestrating learning in classrooms. → [[pedagogy]].
- The self-management side: motivation, habit, procrastination, goal-setting. → [[self-regulation]].
- The measurement and feedback side: testing as evaluation, calibration of self-assessment. → [[assessment]].

When an entry sits at the boundary (e.g., a study technique whose effectiveness is grounded in working-memory limits), assign multiple domains rather than forcing a single choice.

## Canonical questions

The questions that would, if answered, give this domain its load-bearing knowledge:

- What conditions reliably produce durable encoding vs. encoding that decays in days?
- How does the cost-vs-benefit profile of difficulty during practice change with expertise?
- Under what conditions does interleaving help, and when does it interfere?
- How does transfer scale with the distance between training and target contexts?
- What is the relationship between metacognitive accuracy and learning outcomes?
