# The Model Student Who Got a D

**Concepts illustrated:** [[illusions-of-competence]], [[elaboration]]

A first-year psychology student visited his professor in distress. He'd attended every lecture, taken diligent notes, read the text, highlighted critical passages, and reviewed his highlighted material until he felt thoroughly familiar with everything. He got a D. When the professor asked whether he'd tested himself on key concepts, rephrased ideas in his own words, connected them to prior knowledge, or looked for examples outside the text — the answer was no in every case.

## Why it matters

This is the canonical illustration of how "feeling familiar" is not "knowing." The student was diligent but strategically illiterate — he didn't know how to study effectively.
