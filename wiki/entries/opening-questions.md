# Opening Questions

**A retrieval routine for the first few minutes of class.** Before beginning a lecture or activity, ask students to recall — orally or (better) in writing — what the class covered previously. The instructor does not summarize the last class; the students do.

## How to do it

A small menu of opening prompts, in ascending order of cognitive load:

- "Can anyone remind me what we talked about in class on Monday?"
- "Before I introduce our third major theory, what were the two we've covered so far?"
- "We've seen several experiments this semester. Can someone summarize the results we observed?"
- Short written warm-up: three to five minutes, students write what they remember from the last class or from homework reading. Collect or don't — the cognitive work happens either way, via [[covert-retrieval]] for the ones not writing.

A formalized variant (per Annie Blazer, religious studies): one rotating student delivers a 3-5 minute summary of the prior class at the start of each session; each student does this at least once per semester.

## Why it works

1. **Retrieval practice** in its cheapest possible form. Cf. [[testing-effect]].
2. **Discussion priming.** Lang notes he originally used opening writing prompts to jump-start discussion; only later realized they were also potent learning events.
3. **Reaches the whole room.** Even students who don't raise a hand get retrieval practice *if the instructor leaves a pause*; see [[covert-retrieval]].

## Traps to avoid

- **Notebook flipping.** Students will reach for their notes. Tell them explicitly: close the notebook. The retrieval benefit requires drawing from memory, not pattern-matching on notes.
- **Taking the first hand.** Answering immediately shortcuts retrieval for everyone else. Pause for several seconds, which will feel longer than it is.
- **Expecting early fluency.** The first few weeks, students will stare blankly at questions about material you spent hours preparing. This is normal — it reveals (rather than creates) the gap. Persist.
