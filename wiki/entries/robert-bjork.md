# Robert Bjork

Psychologist who, with Elizabeth Bjork, coined the term "desirable difficulty" and conducted foundational research on [[interleaving]], [[spaced-repetition]], and the conditions under which learning is most durable. His work establishes that effortful, laborious learning strategies — despite feeling less productive — produce dramatically superior long-term retention and transfer compared to easy, fluent strategies.

## Key contributions

- Coined "desirable difficulty" with Elizabeth Bjork → [[desirable-difficulty]]
- Research on interleaving as a desirable difficulty → [[interleaving]]
- Distinction between momentary strength and underlying habit strength
- Demonstrated that learners are poor judges of their own learning effectiveness
