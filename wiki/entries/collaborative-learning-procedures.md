# Collaborative Learning Procedures

A set of standard procedures for structuring group work so that collaborative learning produces its cognitive and socioemotional benefits rather than devolving into dysfunction.

## How it works

The procedures operate at three levels: group formation, activity execution, and post-activity processing. Each level leverages neuroscience principles — reducing cognitive load through clear structure, maintaining eustress through time pressure and accountability, and building procedural social skills through repetition.

**Group formation:** Teacher-assigned groups of 3-4 (not self-selected). Shoulder partners for quick tasks; two pairs merge for larger tasks. Groups are reformed each marking period based on observed strengths, personalities, and social dynamics. Avoid pairing chatty students, placing outsiders next to known bullies, or letting students self-segregate.

**Activity execution — six steps:**
1. Assign the task with a written checklist to support working memory
2. Specify time with a visible countdown timer — use atypical increments (e.g., 4 minutes, not 5) so students treat it as a real deadline
3. Follow up before students start: "What are you supposed to do? How much time do you have?"
4. Circulate to monitor — teacher presence alone keeps groups on task
5. Use the "three before me" rule — students check with three peers before asking the teacher
6. Provide a transition cue (30-second warning) to wrap up and prepare for whole-class discussion

**Behavioral expectations:** Address members by name, remain with the group, take turns speaking (rotate every 30-60 seconds), actively listen, maintain appropriate noise level, disagree civilly (criticize ideas not people), integrate everyone's contributions.

**Roles for reading-based tasks:** Previewer (predictions, tracking), Clarifier (vocabulary, comprehension), Connector (text-to-self/world/other-texts), Summarizer (key concepts, new insights for debrief).
