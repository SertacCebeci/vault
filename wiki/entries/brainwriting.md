# Brainwriting

A hybrid brainstorming technique that compensates for the creativity-suppressing effects of large groups by having participants first generate ideas individually and silently, then pool and augment those ideas as a group.

## How it works

Participants brainstorm alone first, writing down their ideas without judgment. The group then comes together, shares all individual ideas, and proceeds to a second stage of conventional brainstorming to build on the combined list. This two-phase structure addresses research showing that people in larger groups generate fewer ideas and become less receptive to outside input compared to when they work alone or in smaller groups.

The individual-first phase protects divergent thinking from social conformity pressure and production blocking (waiting for a turn to speak). The group phase then adds the benefits of association and elaboration. The result is both more ideas and more varied ideas than either phase alone would produce.
