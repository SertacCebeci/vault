# Stanislas Dehaene

Stanislas Dehaene is a leading neuroscientist who developed the [[neuronal-recycling-hypothesis]], which explains how the brain acquires [[biologically-primary-and-secondary-knowledge|biologically secondary]] abilities like reading and mathematics. His research demonstrates that secondary skills systematically invade specific brain regions — letters colonize the occipitotemporal cortex, numbers colonize the intraparietal cortex — regardless of culture or writing system. He concludes that while the brain is plastic, it is not super-plastic: new skills are constrained by preexisting neural architecture.

## Key contributions

- [[neuronal-recycling-hypothesis]] — the theory that secondary learning repurposes brain regions evolved for related primary functions
- Constrained plasticity — the insight that [[neuroplasticity]] operates within anatomical limits
- Brain as prediction machine — argues that generating predictions, detecting errors, and correcting oneself are "the very foundation of effective learning" → [[prediction-as-learning]]
