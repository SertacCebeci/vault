# Mental Models

A mental model is a mental representation of some external reality — a connected web of knowledge that allows you to interpret situations, make decisions, and act fluently. Mental models are built from interconnected [[chunking|chunks]] and shaped through practice, reflection, and [[elaboration]]. An expert is someone who has constructed rich, accurate mental models in their domain and can deploy them rapidly under pressure.

## Elaboration

A baseball batter facing a pitch has less than an instant to decide what's coming. He does it not through one monolithic skill, but through a set of interconnected mental models: models of pitch types (built from cues like the pitcher's windup and the ball's spin), connected to models of batting mechanics, strike zones, game situations, and opponent tendencies. In a great at-bat, all these models fire together seamlessly. This is what expertise looks like — many small knowledge units wired into an integrated whole.

Mental models differ from isolated facts in a crucial way: they're *operational*. A pilot who has memorized that a Cessna 401's single-engine ceiling depends on weight and fuel load has a fact. A pilot who can instantly assess those variables in a crisis, predict whether he can maintain altitude, and choose the correct landing approach has a mental model. The model includes the facts but also the relationships between them, the conditions under which they apply, and the procedures they imply.

Two subskills help mental models form. [[structure-building]] is the ability to identify the foundational ideas in new material and organize them into a scaffold. [[rule-learning]] is the ability to abstract the underlying principle that links multiple cases. Mental models are what those skills produce over time: knowledge with enough structure and logic to be used.

People who learn to extract the underlying principles or rules that differentiate types of problems - rather than memorizing surface features - build better mental models. This skill is better acquired through [[interleaving|interleaved and varied practice]] than through [[overlearning|massed practice]] on a single type.

With enough effortful practice, a complex set of interrelated ideas or a sequence of motor skills fuses into a meaningful whole, forming a mental model somewhat akin to a "brain app." Learning to drive a car involves a host of simultaneous actions that require all of our concentration and dexterity while learning them. But over time, these combinations of cognition and motor skills become ingrained as sets of mental models associated with driving — deeply entrenched and highly efficient skills that, like habits, can be adapted and applied in varied circumstances. Expert performance is built through thousands of hours of practice in your area of expertise, in varying conditions, through which you accumulate a vast library of such mental models.

## Connections

- Builds on: [[chunking]] — mental models are networks of interconnected chunks
- Related to: [[elaboration]] — elaboration builds the connections between chunks that make mental models operational
- Related to: [[transfer]] — rich mental models in one domain can sometimes illuminate problems in another
- Related to: [[interleaving]] — varied practice builds more flexible mental models than blocked practice
- Contrasts with: [[einstellung]] — rigid or oversimplified mental models can block better solutions
- Related to: [[curse-of-knowledge]] — deep mental models become invisible to the expert, making it hard to teach or diagnose novice confusion
- Related to: [[calibration]] — the ability to recognize when a mental model doesn't fit the current situation
- Related to: [[recall-as-learning]] - retrieval practice strengthens and refines mental models
- Related to: [[reconsolidation]] - effortful recall integrates learning into mental models through reconsolidation
- Related to: [[varied-practice]] - varied contexts build more versatile mental models
- Related to: [[desirable-difficulty]] - the effort of building mental models is the productive difficulty
- Related to: [[structure-building]] - structure building identifies the foundational pieces and arranges them into a usable scaffold
- Related to: [[rule-learning]] - rule learning supplies the principles that make a model operational rather than merely descriptive
