# Self-Regulation

## Lead

The inner loop of learning: how a learner manages attention, motivation, procrastination, and habit; how accurate (or inaccurate) their self-assessment of progress is; and how strategy choice and effort allocation respond to feedback. Covers metacognition, goal-setting, the procrastination/intention gap, deliberate practice as a self-managed activity.

## Scope

This domain covers learner-side processes that shape how learning effort is allocated:

- Metacognition — knowledge of one's own cognition; calibration of self-assessment.
- Goal-setting and intention — the gap between intention and action.
- Procrastination, self-control, and time management.
- Habit formation and the role of cue-routine-reward loops in study practice.
- Motivation: intrinsic vs. extrinsic, expectancy-value, self-determination theory.
- Mindset and beliefs about ability (growth/fixed mindset literature, with attention to replication issues).
- Interleaved use of focus modes — deliberate attention vs. mind-wandering / diffuse mode.
- Effort regulation across long arcs (semesters, expertise development).

## Out of scope

- Externally orchestrated instructional structures (course design, classroom routines) → [[pedagogy]].
- Cognitive mechanisms studied independently of learner choice (e.g., spacing as a memory operation rather than as a study strategy a learner chooses) → [[learning-theory]].
- Clinical attention or motivation disorders (ADHD treatment, depression's effect on learning).

## Canonical questions

- How accurate is metacognitive self-assessment in untrained adults, and what interventions improve calibration?
- What proportion of learners' chosen study strategies align with evidence-based recommendations, and what blocks the gap from closing?
- Under what conditions do intention-implementation interventions (implementation intentions, planning prompts) produce durable behavior change?
- How does expert deliberate practice differ from novice study, and what's transferable from one to the other?
- What's the evidence base for growth-mindset interventions after the 2010s replication crisis?
