# Eric Mazur

Physicist and educator at Harvard University, best known for developing the [[peer-instruction]] teaching method. Mazur articulated the [[curse-of-knowledge]] problem in teaching: "The better you know something, the more difficult it becomes to teach it." His insight that students who have recently learned material are often better at explaining it than experts (because they haven't yet lost access to the state of not-knowing) led him to develop a teaching method that leverages peer discussion.

## Key contributions
- [[peer-instruction]] — classroom method integrating quick conceptual tests with small-group peer discussion; engages students in underlying concepts and reveals comprehension gaps
- [[curse-of-knowledge]] — articulated how expert mental models become invisible, making effective teaching harder as expertise deepens
