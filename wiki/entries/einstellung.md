# Einstellung

Einstellung (German for "mindset" or "installation") is the phenomenon where an initial thought, an existing idea, or a well-developed neural pattern prevents you from finding a better solution. In the pinball analogy of [[focused-and-diffuse-modes]], the initial ball of thought goes to the upper part of the brain (familiar territory), but the solution lives in the lower part — and the crowded bumpers of the focused mode create a rut that prevents the ball from reaching the new place.

## Elaboration

Einstellung is essentially a cognitive roadblock installed by your own expertise. The more strongly you've developed a particular approach, the more it can block alternative solutions. This has practical implications at every level: students who learn one problem-solving technique may try to force it onto problems where a different technique is needed; scientists may be unable to see paradigm-shifting evidence because their existing frameworks filter it out. Thomas Kuhn observed that paradigm shifts in science are often driven by young people or people trained in a different discipline — precisely because they lack the entrenched patterns that produce Einstellung.

The remedy is the [[focused-and-diffuse-modes|diffuse mode]]: when you're stuck, step back and let your mind wander. The diffuse mode's wider, semi-random connections can bypass the blocked pathways. [[interleaving]] also helps by training you to flexibly select among approaches rather than defaulting to a single one.

There is an interesting connection to [[working-memory]]: people with larger working memories may be *more* susceptible to Einstellung because their prefrontal cortex holds existing thoughts so tightly that new ideas can't break through. Those with smaller working memories — or ADHD-like attention shifts — may actually have a creative advantage.
