# Daniel Willingham

Cognitive psychologist at the University of Virginia, author of *Why Don't Students Like School?* (2009). His central argument is that [[knowledge-as-foundation]] — factual knowledge stored in long-term memory — is essential for all higher-order cognitive skills. "You can't think creatively about information unless you have information in your head to think about." His work also emphasizes that "memory is the residue of thought" — we remember what we spend time thinking about, which grounds strategies like [[prediction-as-learning]] and [[elaboration]].

## Key contributions
- Argument that factual knowledge is the prerequisite for critical thinking, not its opposite
- "Memory is the residue of thought" — the principle that active cognitive engagement determines what gets remembered
