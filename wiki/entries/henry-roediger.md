# Henry L. Roediger III

Cognitive psychologist at Washington University in St. Louis, where he runs the **Memory Lab** — the research group that has produced much of the modern experimental literature on the [[testing-effect]]. Co-author of *Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning* (2014) with Peter Brown and Mark McDaniel — the book that translated decades of memory research into advice for students and teachers.

## Key contributions

- Co-designed the **2007 art-history experiment** with Andrew Butler that compared short-answer, multiple-choice, restudy, and no-activity conditions over a 30-day interval; see [[roediger-butler-art-history]]. Still the cleanest single demonstration that production-format retrieval outperforms recognition-format retrieval.
- Led (with research associate Pooja Agarwal and teacher Patrice Bain) the Columbia, Illinois middle-school quizzing program that moved the testing effect out of the lab and into an ordinary classroom; see [[columbia-il-science-quizzing]].
- Co-authored the covert-retrieval study (Pyc, Agarwal, Roediger 2014) showing that silent retrieval is roughly as effective as overt; see [[covert-retrieval]].

## Orientation

Roediger's long-running argument: tests are not merely measurement instruments. They are themselves among the most powerful learning events available, and the education system has badly underused them by treating them as assessment rather than instruction.
