# Essay — When to Promote Borderline-Notability Units

## Lead

Borderline-notability units that pick up a single inbound wikilink from a *different* chapter (not the chapter that staged them) are usually worth promoting. The intuition is that the second chapter's editor independently arrived at the same concept, which is itself a weak form of routing necessity.

## The observation

§8.1's notability predicate names three sufficient conditions: multi-source coverage, routing necessity (≥2 inbound), or policy carve-out. A single inbound link does not meet routing necessity at v0. But in practice, when one chapter stages a unit as borderline (e.g., a one-source aside the editor was unsure about) and a *later, independent* chapter wikilinks to it, that link is evidence that the unit is real — the second editor was reasoning about the same subject.

## A proposed heuristic

Promote a borderline unit at the first inbound link from a different chapter, regardless of whether routing necessity (N=2) is technically met. Rationale:

- The cost of promoting a borderline unit is low: a normal entry slot, the closeout machinery, a possible advisory finding if it later orphans.
- The cost of *not* promoting and waiting for a second inbound is the unit retiring at 90 days while still being plausibly useful.
- A second link, even if eventually never coming, signals the topic has cross-source viability the original ingestion missed.

## Counterargument

The threshold N=2 exists precisely to filter out one-off references that look load-bearing in the moment but never accumulate. A heuristic that promotes at N=1 risks accreting orphaned almost-entries.

The compromise: promote at one inbound only when the linking entry is a structure note or insight entry (i.e., entries whose work *is* organizing, so an inbound from one of them is stronger evidence than from a typical concept).

## Status

Essay. Binds nothing. Promotion to guideline would happen if a few dozen instances of one-link promotion produce demonstrably better cluster coverage than waiting for the second link.
