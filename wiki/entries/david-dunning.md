# David Dunning

Social psychologist known for research on self-assessment and metacognition. With Justin Kruger, he published the landmark 1999 paper "Unskilled and Unaware of It," establishing the [[dunning-kruger-effect]] — the finding that incompetent people systematically overestimate their competence because they lack the skills to recognize their deficiency. Dunning argues that the path to self-insight leads through other people and objective feedback, not introspection.

## Key contributions
- [[dunning-kruger-effect]] — incompetent people overestimate competence; the same skill needed to perform well is the skill needed to recognize good performance
- Research showing that domain-specific training (not general encouragement) improves metacognitive accuracy
- Advocacy for "motivated reasoning" as a core obstacle to self-knowledge — people have a "sheer genius" for convincing themselves of congenial conclusions
