# Michael Ullman

Neuroscientist at Georgetown University who developed the groundbreaking declarative-procedural theory of language learning. His framework demonstrates that both the [[declarative-learning-system]] and [[procedural-learning-system]] contribute to language acquisition, each handling different aspects — the declarative system for vocabulary and rules, the procedural system for grammar and fluency.

## Key contributions

- Declarative-procedural model of language learning → [[procedural-learning-system]], [[declarative-learning-system]]
- Research showing that both learning systems can and often do learn information related to the same topic → [[declarative-procedural-seesaw]]
- Each learning system has types of information it is particularly good at acquiring
