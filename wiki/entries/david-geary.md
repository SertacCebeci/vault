# David Geary

David Geary is an evolutionary educational psychologist who developed the framework distinguishing [[biologically-primary-and-secondary-knowledge]]. His work bridges evolutionary psychology and education, arguing that some knowledge (face recognition, language) is biologically primary — wired by evolution — while other knowledge (reading, math) is biologically secondary and requires deliberate instruction. He emphasizes that secondary learning requires extensive practice distributed over time because the neural links "are not naturally very strong."

## Key contributions

- [[biologically-primary-and-secondary-knowledge]] — the primary/secondary framework for understanding why some subjects are harder to learn
- [[direct-instruction]] — quoted on the importance of distributed practice for secondary learning
