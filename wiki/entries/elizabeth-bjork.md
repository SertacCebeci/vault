# Elizabeth Bjork

Cognitive psychologist at UCLA, known (along with [[robert-bjork]]) for research on [[desirable-difficulty]] and effective learning strategies. Her classroom pretesting experiments demonstrated that giving students short multiple-choice pretests on material not yet covered in lecture improved their performance on those topics by approximately 10% on the final exam — even though students performed at chance on the pretests themselves. This work provided key real-world evidence for [[prediction-as-learning]].

## Key contributions
- Pretesting experiments in introductory psychology courses demonstrating prediction's classroom effectiveness
- [[desirable-difficulty]] — co-developer of the concept with Robert Bjork
