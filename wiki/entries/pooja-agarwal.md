# Pooja K. Agarwal

Cognitive psychologist and the most visible contemporary advocate for retrieval practice in K-12 and higher education. Trained in Henry Roediger's Memory Lab at Washington University in St. Louis, where as a research associate she worked directly with middle- and high-school teachers to bring retrieval-practice research into real classrooms.

## Key contributions

- Co-designed and ran, with sixth-grade teacher Patrice Bain, the Columbia, Illinois middle-school retrieval-practice program that produced a full-letter-grade improvement on quizzed vs. non-quizzed material; see [[columbia-il-science-quizzing]].
- Co-author with Bain of *Powerful Teaching: Unleashing the Science of Learning* (2019) — a translation of retrieval-practice research for classroom teachers.
- Runs **RetrievalPractice.org**, which aggregates research, classroom strategies, and practitioner-facing write-ups.
- Contributed to the covert-retrieval study (Pyc, Agarwal, Roediger 2014); see [[covert-retrieval]].

## Orientation

Agarwal's central message, quoted at length in [[small-teaching]] Ch. 2: retrieval practice **works because it is hard**. "Slower, effortful retrieval leads to long-term learning. In contrast, fast, easy strategies only lead to short-term learning." She is unusually sharp about naming the counterintuitive part — that the feeling of struggle during retrieval is exactly what students (and teachers) mistake for failure, when in fact it is the learning signal itself.
