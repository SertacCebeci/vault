**Key themes:** The chapter's central argument is that the biggest obstacle to effective learning is not lack of effort but poor metacognition - the inability to accurately judge what you know. The cardinal rule: practice like you play, because you will play like you practice. This chapter completes the book's argument arc: Ch. 1-4 established *what* works and *why*; Ch. 5 explains why we resist what works and prefer what doesn't.
